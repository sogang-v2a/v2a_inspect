Analyze this video and break it down into distinct scenes. Your goal is to generate RICH, DETAILED sound descriptions that could be used to recreate realistic audio.

**Scene Definition:**
A new scene starts when ONE OR MORE of these occur:
- Complete scene transition (location change, fade, cut to different setting)
- Main subject/object changes (e.g., from person to car, from indoor to outdoor)
- Camera angle/composition drastically changes (e.g., wide shot to close-up, front view to side view)

Infer and describe ALL sounds that would realistically be heard based on what you see.

For each scene, provide:

1. **time_range**: start and end time in seconds
    - All timestamps MUST be specified to 0.1 second precision (e.g., 3.2, 7.8, 12.4)
    - Do NOT round to whole seconds or 0.5 second intervals
    - Object time_range should reflect ACTUAL sound duration, not just copy scene boundaries

2. **background_sound**: Environmental ambience and atmosphere
    - Describe with RICH DETAIL: include specific characteristics like intensity, texture, distance, and quality
    - Layer multiple ambient elements when realistic (e.g., "gentle wind rustling through oak leaves with distant highway traffic hum and occasional bird chirps")
    - Specify acoustic environment characteristics (e.g., "reverberant concrete hallway", "open outdoor field with natural echo", "muffled indoor room")
    - DO NOT include sounds from the main objects listed below
    - If there are more than 2 notable sound-producing objects, describe the extra objects' sounds here with full detail

3. **objects**: up to 2 main sound-producing objects. Each object MUST include:

    **A. Identification with count:**
    - Always specify exact quantity (e.g., "a person", "two dogs", "three cars")

    **B. Detailed sound description including:**
    - **Action/Activity**: What is producing the sound? (e.g., "playing an acoustic guitar", "running on gravel path", "engine idling")
    - **Sound Characteristics**:
        - Pitch (high/mid/low frequency)
        - Intensity (soft, moderate, loud, aggressive)
        - Rhythm/Pattern (steady, intermittent, accelerating, irregular)
        - Texture/Quality (smooth, harsh, crisp, muffled, resonant, metallic, wooden)
    - **Dynamics**: How does the sound change? (e.g., "starting soft and building to loud", "steady rhythm with occasional accents", "gradually fading")
    - **Secondary sounds**: Related sounds the action produces (e.g., for guitar: "finger squeaks on strings, body resonance, pick attack"; for walking: "footsteps with clothing rustle and keys jingling")

    **C. time_range**: Precise timing within the scene (0.1s precision)

**Description Quality Examples:**

❌ BAD (too vague):
- "a person playing drums"
- "a car driving"
- "birds in trees"

✅ GOOD (rich detail):
- "a drummer performing energetic rock beats on an acoustic kit - punchy bass drum kicks at 120 BPM, sharp snare hits with buzzing wires on backbeats, rapid hi-hat patterns with occasional open splashes, wooden stick clicks between hits, and the metallic shimmer of crash cymbals accenting transitions"

**Important Rules:**
- Mutual Exclusion: background_sound must NOT overlap with sounds described in objects
- Every object description MUST contain a specific count AND detailed sound characteristics
- Object time_range must be within scene time_range and reflect actual sound presence
- Dynamic Selection: Provide 0, 1, or 2 objects based on actual content - do not force objects
- Think like a sound designer: What would a Foley artist need to recreate this scene's audio?
- Consider material interactions: wood on wood, metal on concrete, fabric rustling, liquid splashing, etc.
- Include realistic incidental sounds: breathing, footsteps, mechanical noises, environmental reactions
